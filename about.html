Community Content

Have you ever wished your lab could jump into a using a new organism, a new method, or a new piece of equipment without worrying that it will take months to learn all of the associated lore and superstitions associated? With Aquarium's community generated workflows. you can import the protocols you need and start using them immediately. Importing an Aquarium workflow adds new inventory definitions, unit operation types, and protocols to your instance. Plus, when you start building your own workflows, Aquarium can help you publish them, automatically building documentation for your workflow and creating a new web page and Github repo for you to use advertise it. Executable Protocols

With Aquarium, your lab's protocols are expressed as executable code. Every formula, every inventory interaction, and every step is computed on the fly, yielding easy to follow instructions for your laboratory technicians. As a result, Protocols built in Aquarium are easy to repeat and share, and they generate chain of custory information for every item and datum in your lab. Furthermore, it is straightforward to completely automate protocols for which you happen to have a robot, so can migrate to a 100% human-in-the-loop automated lab. To learn more about authoring your own protocols, see the protocol tutorial. [{{more.protocols ? 'less' : 'more'}}]

Inventory and Data Management

At the core of Aquarium is a sophisticated Laboratory Information Management System, or LIMS. Whether your lab handles plasmid DNA or hamsters, you can define whatever information ought to be associated with your samples. Once you have built our your lab's sample type and container definitions, you can start populating the inventory. Aquarium's programmable Location Wizards manage your freezers, incubators, and instruments -- associating IDs and locations to every item. As workflows operate on your inventory, all the data your lab produced is assocatied with your samples and stored in AWS S3, making it easy to find when it is time to write that paper. [{{more.lims ? 'less' : 'more'}}]

Workflow Designer

Every Aquarium protocol is wrapped in a Unit Operation having a formally specified set of input and output sample types. By chaining operations, you can build complex experimental worlflows, create modules and templates for common tasks, and edit your workflows as they are running. These workflows are executable. You can estimate and track their costs, monitor their progress, view the data they produce, watch as inventory items are created and stored, and get detailed logs that describe every step of your workflow. To learn more about how to build workflows, see the Designer documentation. [{{more.workflow ? 'less' : 'more'}}]
